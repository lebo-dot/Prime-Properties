Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions? We have answers! Here you'll find the most common inquiries and our responses.

1. What are the steps to buy a house?

To buy a house, start with determining your budget, getting pre-approved for a mortgage, and finding a real estate agent. After that, begin house-hunting, make an offer on a property, and, once accepted, proceed with inspections and appraisals before closing.

2. How do I sell my property faster?

To sell your property faster, focus on curb appeal, price it competitively, and consider minor upgrades to make it more appealing. Also, hiring an experienced real estate agent can help market it effectively.

3. How much down payment is required?

Typically, a down payment can range from 5% to 20% of the property's price, depending on the type of mortgage. Some loans may even offer options with as little as 3% down for qualified buyers.

4. What is the role of a real estate agent?

A real estate agent helps clients buy or sell properties, offering expertise on market trends, property values, and negotiation strategies. They guide clients through each step, from listing to closing.

5. What are closing costs, and who pays them?

Closing costs include fees for appraisals, inspections, taxes, and legal services. Both buyers and sellers may have specific fees to cover, but typically, buyers cover most closing costs unless otherwise negotiated.
